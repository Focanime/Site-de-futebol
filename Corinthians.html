Club History

Sport Club Corinthians Paulista was founded on September 1, 1910.
The name was defined later, in honor of the English team Corinthian FC,
who was touring Brazil at the time. Other name ideas were Santos
Dumont and Carlos Gomes. The team is the greatest champion in the history of the
São Paulo Championship, with 26 cups. Corinthians still has a world title, four
Brazilian Championships and three Cups in Brazil. One of the most celebrated
achievements by fans were the 1977 State, with the famous goal by Basilio,
breaking a taboo of 22 years and eight months without titles.
